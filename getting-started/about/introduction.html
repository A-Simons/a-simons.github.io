Introduction

CFRMIS, the Community Fire Risk Management Information System, is the database that we use to manage fire risk throughout the county, across our Prevention, Protection and Operational teams.

CFRMIS can be used for keeping track of our Home Fire Safety Checks, our Fire Protection activity under the Fire Safety Order, #OTHER EXAMPLES# and #OTHER EXAMPLES#. Depending on your role, you will have access to some or all of these functions.

CFRMIS is not an in-house application, it has been developed in response to the needs of dozens of fire and rescue services across the country and can record a great deal of information, a lot of which we in NFRS do not use. This guide is focused on making sure that we get the most out of CFRMIS and that it can be an effective tool to help us keep the people of Northamptonshire safer.

Who uses CFRMIS?

CFRMIS is used by the Prevention team to coordinate and record community fire safety activity throughout the county, working directly with members of the public to help them make their own homes safer.

The Protection team use CFRMIS to support their activity as regulators of fire safety in commercial premises, working with persons with legal reponsibility for fire safety in buildings other than domestic premises.

Operations support both of these functions, carrying out Home Fire Safety Checks and HMO Fire Safety checks, working directly with occupiers and landlords to improve fire safety where people live.

  • CFRMIS is a a complex database, with scope to record information relating to Domestic Premises, Commerical Premises, Community Fire Safety activity, Technical Fire Safety, communications with members of the public and activites carried out by NFRS staff and our partners.

  • It is web-based and can be accessed in a range of web browsers from NFRS sites, or remotely using a remote desktop connection.

CFRMIS is capable of recording and managing a huge range of information, but it isn’t always the most intuitive system to use.

This guide is intended to help you to understand how CFRMIS works, and to make sure that we’re all using it consistently.
